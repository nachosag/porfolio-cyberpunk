ENGINEERING MINDSET

I'm a backend developer passionate about system design and the kind of problems that don't have obvious solutions. I gravitate toward architecture decisions, data modeling, and the mechanics of making things fast, reliable, and maintainable.

My work spans from building complete REST APIs with FastAPI and Express.js, to implementing algorithms like K-means clustering from scratch. I believe in understanding what happens under the hood — not just wiring libraries together.

Currently leveling up in distributed systems and exploring the intersection of ML pipelines with production backend infrastructure.

# Engineering Manifesto
 
1. Understand the problem BEFORE
touching the keyboard.
 
2. A system is only as good as its
weakest abstraction.
 
3. Complexity is the enemy.
Simplicity is earned.
 
4. Don't just use libraries.
Know what they're doing.
 
5. Production systems have no mercy.
Your architecture better.
 
$ _
◈

CLEAN ARCHITECTURE

Separation of concerns isn't a suggestion. Layers exist for a reason — and violating them always costs you later.

⊞

SYSTEMS THINKING

Every component is part of a larger system. Understanding dependencies and failure modes before writing line one.

⊿

ALGORITHMIC DEPTH

Knowing when NOT to use a library is as important as knowing how to use one. Understand the algorithm first.

◉

DELIBERATE EXPERIMENTATION

Projects like backend-lab exist because patterns need to be tested, broken, and rebuilt before they're production-ready.

⬡

DATA-CENTRIC DESIGN

The data model is the system. Get the schema wrong and everything built on top of it becomes technical debt.

ESTABLISH CONNECTION

GET IN TOUCH

Open to remote backend engineering roles. Let's talk about systems, architecture, or interesting problems worth solving.
